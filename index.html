About me

I am a Postdoc in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, working with Prof. John C.S. Lui. Prior to this, I was a Postdoc in the Department of Electronic and Computer Engineering at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST), working with Prof. Danny H.K. Tsang. I also obtained my Ph.D. degree from HKUST.

Among others, from September 2017 to February 2018, I visited the Department of Industrial Engineering and Operations Research at Columbia Univerisity, and worked with Prof. David Yao and Prof. Ward Whitt. From Octorber 2019 to April 2020, I visited the Department of Computing and Mathematical Sciences at the California Institute of Technology, and worked with Prof. Steven Low and Prof. Adam Wierman. I also work closely with Prof. Mohammad Hajiesmaili at the University of Massachusetts Amherst.

Research interests

I work on optimization and decision-making under uncertainty for networked systems, such as energy-transport nexus (e.g., electric vehical networks, e-mobility), cloud/edge computing platforms, and smart grids. I am most excited to build novel models to understand the fundamental limits/trade-offs of real-world applications (e.g., the first queueing network model for battery swapping stations), and develop performance-guaranteed algorithms to push the theoretical boundaries of classic problems (e.g., optimal competitive algorithm for online knapsack problems).
