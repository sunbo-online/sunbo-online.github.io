About me

I am a postdoc in the Department of Electronic and Computer Engineering at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST), working with Prof. Danny H.K. Tsang. Before this, I received my Ph.D. from HKUST in December 2018 and my B.En. from the Honors School of Harbin Institute of Technology (HIT) in July 2013. From September 2017 to February 2018, I visited the Department of Industrial Engineering and Operations Research at Columbia Univerisity, working with Prof. David Yao and Prof. Ward Whitt. From Octorber 2019 to April 2020, I visited the Department of Computing and Mathematical Sciences at California Institute of Technology and worked with Prof. Steven Low and Prof. Adam Wierman.

Research interests

I currently work on algorithms and theory for online optimization to impact the sustainability, effectiveness, and robustness of real-world networked systems. The ultimate goal of my research is (i) to identify new motivating applications (e.g., online EV charging) that can be enabled or improved by new online optimization approaches, and (ii) to develop algorithms and theory that can unify the existing online optimization approaches into systematic design frameworks (e.g., online primal-dual algorithms).
