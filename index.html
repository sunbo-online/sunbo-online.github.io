Research interests

I work at the intersection of theory and systems, focusing on developing algorithms with provable guarantees for online decision-making problems in networked systems, such as energy systems (electric vehicle networks and smart grids) and computing systems (cloud computing and mobile edge computing). I am recently interested in the following topics:
• Online competitive algorithms that provide robust online decisions with worst-case guarantees

  • Online knapsack problems (SIGMETRICS23)
  • Online conversion problems (SIGMETRICS21)
• Learning-augmented algorithms that leverage predictions from AI/ML tools in algorithm design
  • Consistency-robustness analysis (e-Energy24 and NeurIPS21)
  • Distributionally robust competitive analysis (ICML24)
• Algorithmic fairness that encodes fairness in online algorithms and mechanism design
  • Time fairness (WINE24 and ICLR24)
  • Group fairness (SIGMETRICS25)

About me

I am an Assistant Professor at the School of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at the University of Ottawa. Previously, I was a postdoc at the University of Waterloo with Prof. Raouf Boutaba and at The Chinese University of Hong Kong with Prof. John C.S. Lui. I also worked with Prof. Danny H.K. Tsang at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST), where I obtained my Ph.D. degree. Among others, I was a visiting scholar at Columbia Univerisity, working with Prof. David Yao and Prof. Ward Whitt. I also visited the California Institute of Technology, and worked with Prof. Steven Low and Prof. Adam Wierman.
